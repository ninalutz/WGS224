Introduction

Roe V Wade is the court case in 1973 that legalized abortion in the United States. The judges voted 7-2 that a woman’s right to an abortion was guaranteed in the 14th Amendment of the constitution. While some states had legal abortion this legalized it in all 50 states. Since then there have been various court rulings on restrictions that can be put on abortion. One of the biggest since has been Planned Parenthood of Se. Pa. v. Casey, ruling that restrictions can be put on abortion as long as it does not put “undue burden” upon the woman. None of these, however, have overturned the original Roe V Wade.

While states for many years have been putting unconstitutional restrictions upon abortions, the access to abortion has not been at risk like it is now. Judges are hesitant to overturn precedent, but that is now changing. President Donald Trump promised to appoint judges that would overturn Roe V Wade; he has so far appointed two conservative justices, Justices Gorsuch and Kavanaugh. Recently, these two justices were a part of a majority overruling precedent, showing that may no longer protect Roe V Wade. Additionally, many states are passed legislation with the purpose of getting them to the Supreme Court, such as “heartbeat bills” that ban abortion after 6 weeks, before most people would know they are pregnant. These may in the next few years work their way to the Supreme Court, which would give the conservative justices the opportunity to overturn Roe V Wade.

This information has been compiled to show you what the current climate is like and what it would be if this landmark decision was overturned. We have also included a page with direct information on what YOU can do and where you can find this information and more.
